Untitled (Feb 10, 2023)

Twisted crocodiles, three,
Unfloating, invite me in.
Whose temple is this? In comes fire
blue, frozen fire.
I knock on
Eyeballs. They pop out. No
River, sleepiness, cramps,
Neck pain. Stillness in time,
Cursed, sweet blessing.
Pillars slanted, two, one
Headless, the other mocking.
Hands so large, woven in,
Piles of fulfilled, sad-eyed dreams.
Now sing. The pond licks my sandals.
The moon suddenly rotates
And the panther comes out
Demanding lines, to feast on.

Untitled (Feb 12, 2023)

Come lie on the dry mountain
Of words, she says, light up
Its creases and falls.
Expose yourself, the parts
You did not know. Hide the rest.
Like a bird - like a radio wave
Given into a promise:
The universe, as if listening, benevolent,
All-absorptive.
When I care about them then
So does the world.
I run down a hill pretending
As wind until I hit a wall.
Then I go into those cracks,
You see, they sharpen,
Like pencil sharpeners.

Untitled (Feb 13, 2023)

In evening, before the outburst
The kiln blushes, smoke
Around its eyes, pressurized.
Do not say thank you. Ward it off.
Better to run out of light - and sit
In a dark corner, waiting.
Until
Each strand with its tune
Plucked out of dusty roadsides
Lifts - some drifting - some
Bleeding - and twists,
Compelled.

Untitled (Feb 17, 2023)

A drip of fear spoils it all, that cannot be!
Bring back the sand, beacon, and steps
over shallow seawater - just squint,
You can see it in the dishes smeared
with grease, in the dust collecting by
the toilet paper, in yourself with skin
washed over by city air, smelling of
sweat and smoke.
You see it, sometimes, in lichen on the
north side of rocks. You may exchange
secret glances with those little arms of
spores.
Remember a day you once lived for -
fair weather, a Task, a friend who
listens. Why can’t that day be the best
of one’s life? The whole of past and
future, all for that - a clear view of joy.

To think (Feb 22, 2023)

To think, up to that point,
A life story-less, preferring
Thick distorting lens
Rather than this, here, now.
My friend, a walk we will have
On rocks, songs inside chest,
And taste emptiness like
Sweet, flowing water.

Procrastinate, now

Procrastinate, now,
Make this scene happen
take what she holds dear and
Grill it to medium-rare.
Sleep on it tomorrow!
Today you have work to do.
They are stuck in a conundrum -
The old man, fixed to a mast
Despairs to the grueling wait.
And we still don’t know
What SHE’ll do, on the other side;
Drop the world now, dear,
Make it happen for once and all.

Defenestrated

Defenestrated ideas crawl
Back to me at night.
“In-cor-por-ate–me”
So I kick the violent ones out
And keep feeding a few,
With blood.

Minimalism

I laugh at myself for
Putting jam and peanut butter
On separate parts of the bread,
Once.

Drunk by sleepiness

So sleepy
I can eat a torch
Heavy, it is, or is it a state of
drunkenness
An e-mail will set it straight, yes
I write, being horizontal.
Think of the things they could say!
What acts they do to each other
And crimes, petty ones, how
Intoxicating, dreams in handcuffs.
They dance under my fingers.
I dance to Sealticge, o cruel one.

Take me back

Take me back to that place, o beloved seat
Where the coffee grinned and the words sparkled
Where the stream sang and
the boat flew
Far, far away from the familiar fear
Which grips like a virus reinstalling itself
The broken program remains broken
I remain seated, looking for a dance.

Grab, then, onto seaweed

Grab, then, onto seaweed,
Anchors have denied their chains
And the storm tips it all,
– still the shed feather floats.
Why did you show this secret?
Fingers are useless now except
For holding into
Curved little fishhooks:
They spin threads, twist by twist
Topped up with more of
Which used to be bland, badly cooked
Nonsense.
In the drawer, the yarn ferments, ready
for the next waking hour;
I wish this would never end, because it
will. All good music ends.
