Some poems are forgotten because what they point to has expired. Still, the shell of each resembles something of value.

Goodbye, everything so far,

Goodbye, everything so far,
Purple things, blue things
Pale apricot and grapes
Mild chocolate, muddy
Olive; fluorescent pranks
And pastel air - warm black
And creamy white; violet
Breathless leaves, orange
Strands or sectioned gray;
A light, light touch of navy
And icy aquamarine; blunt
Pinkish suns, 79 of them
Burning to keep you alive
Invisible red and invisible blue…
Enough of all that, enough,
It’s work time. Paper and pens
You should be my life.

Good night, air of spring

Good night, air of spring
For as many hours away
See me in the marching clouds;
March I must, for the Cat comes as a wind
And evolution too, behind every vowel on the tongue;
There is no need to beg;
Everything is here.

Gravity

A full moon warms
Wind-swept branches
Into my eyes
The same light
At midday
Touching your face
The burning clouds
Connecting us
Across the seas.
Gravity.

Of the endlessly shared

Of the endlessly shared
Morning light
That happened in history
I have yet to speak enough
Before I was here.
It is basically still a secret,
Isn’t it? Hours have been put into
Delicate looping; even leaves
Are no longer green;
People holding hands
Invade my streets.
I would let time run out if
No other way brings winter about;
If I can testify that you exist.

Other people full of words

Other people full of words
Are never given the chance to speak
Water jars with floating flowers
Slowly decaying, giving in to heat
They are carried, so I have heard
To as far as some million miles
And poured into the sea
And left to the waves
Forever untangling. Why me? I ask one pair of eyes, the closest that I have ever
seen.
It is a quarter past waking, and
Words are already draining out of me.

Cusco

Temple of dark gardens and tiny waterfalls
Pillars that twist and bend
Hiding stones fresh with flow
The veil incomprehensible
Stars trapped in cages fragile
May I gently crush those veins of yours?

Good night, morning,

Good night, morning,
A cycle of greetings
A line for making meaning
Away from eyes, playing
In the murky clouds of night,
Sleeping, water around your
Feet, dancing – a sky boat
Singing because of me, since
It carries me to your meeting
Slyly. Birds of time still chirping
Let me correct those things
Everyday, spinning, like a cat
That catches its tail, scratching,
Like a cat looking for company;
Good night, starling, we have
Occupied Earth
Most evenly.

July 13th, on mortality

In the cold forest called limitation
Shall my bones and unrealized love
be buried, still burning, ready for
Rebirth. A necessary trick it is
Indeed, promising the meeting in
The next life with the one far away
In this one.
In the colder sea made of flows
Neither you nor me exist but still
The continuation breathes a light
Sigh: memories take care of each other
So that we shall be defined mutually;
Be symmetric to me.
Until now
Never did not mean never.
And now it does
When you tell me to look ahead.

Terminal Speed

Kid I not, o sacred fire of Pig,
Make ferns burn too if only spores
Would fall, and pollinate the sea
Overrun, trypophobists sinking
And all cliffs banned
Night-time tripled so
Shed were layers of excess
To bare and bear
Photo-decomposition.
To think - lightless treasures
In the landfill.
Until the Big Merge - I hear
They burn all Candles there, fields
Of specks without smoke and
Clouds covering the golden path
I hear that Consciousness cradles you like a baby
I’ll have a word with you then.

Good night, my Cat

Good night, my Cat
The moment fiction harms you
You can quit gracefully.
But not these streets, though
They may SEEM like fiction
Rather convincingly.
To cross a road only because
The signal turns green
To wander in evening town
Only fleeing from ennui.
The symmetry is broken -
I condemn the past me
Is this what you think it is?
What do you want it to be?
For eyes that reach into the sky
Even a hundred years aren’t long
So be comforted, you,
we will meet alive, many times, and soon.
