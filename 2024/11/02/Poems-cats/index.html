I need a cat on this chilled, leafy night

I need a cat on this chilled leafy night,
My shower’s running and I stand, unsure
Of colors that shifted, scattering
jewels
On walks under sun that sought seeds sown;
To try and record so others could remember
Loses to the seeing, the touch, the eyes
I’d rather have a cat recollect
What a person I was, every month on;
Water’s splashing and the sirens ring
How silent this is, floors without paws!
How I wish to convince, or play, or de-thorn
Another creature with dirt in her claws.
To get better at approaching winter -
Carry home again tabby stripes
Or squid ink, snow, sunflower, duster
I need a cat on this chilled, leafy night.

But I have a cat in bed

An incense box I’ve lifted - afterward.
Sparks do fade; smoke runs long.
The stick will crumble in a few or more
The porcelain fail into chips, at last, dust;
Yet I hold the gadget - still warm
A prolonged moment steeped in scent.
Scent of years I won’t elaborate
All you see are fingers tinted - dyed
Scent of unknown - oh wait
he’s here (meow)
Sorry guys, I am off snuggling with the cat.

All my other cats

All my other cats left early.
Only a gray cat with gray eyes
Stares from the kitchen table
At me, knitting wool in gradients
Synchronized to the day.
They’ll come back at the darkest.

All my other cats left early
Because the yarn is not available
What do they expect outside?
At 3 pm, there will be a storm.
And of course now, I know that.
I’ve spared a towel to dry them.

Meow, said him.
He curls into the wine box
Dozes off
His whiskers twitch;
He make me sleepy.
I end up sleeping inside.

Rolling thunders.

I do not wake up when
The gray cat comes to me.

Handsome Sampson, Prince of voids

Isotropic space
Collapsed time
You are the only arrow,
My void.
Uncountable doubts and confusions
And pride clashing on the ground
But - Your head on my hand
Purring.
Trust me,
I’m trying

Rosalind

Most exalted, blazing,
Sharp and round orange of sass
My time was yours
And I’ll never be more honored
Your eyes, teeth,
Your grunts and hisses
The tail, whip-like
The ears, buttery pliable
Each mark is a line
You wrote, violently, unfaltering
Not the type to back off -
Neither was I.
But when you are gentle
It is time for your castle.
In mornings, you will nudge her
Purring, somewhere
else under light.
I think of the day we met
And my wish is fulfilled.
